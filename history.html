The History Of Russia

Origins:
  • In 862, a Varangian (Viking) leader named Rurik was invited to rule over the Slavs, establishing the Rurik dynasty and the first political entity known as Rus
  • In 882, Prince Oleg of Novgorod seized Kiev, uniting northern and southern Slavic lands into what became Kievan Rus’, the first major East Slavic state.
  • In 988, Prince Vladimir the Great converted Rus’ to Christianity, aligning the region culturally with the Byzantine Empire and shaping Russian identity for centuries.
Mongol Invasion:
  • In the early 1200s, the Mongol Empire invaded.
  • By 1240, Kiev fell, and the region came under the control of the Golden Horde, a Mongol successor state.
  • This period weakened Kievan Rus’, causing political fragmentation but also setting the stage for new centers of power to rise in the north.
The Russian Empire:
  • In 1721, after major military victories and reforms, Peter the Great proclaimed the Russian Empire.
  • Russia expanded massively across Siberia, Eastern Europe, and into the Caucasus and Central Asia.
  • The empire became one of the world’s largest and most powerful states.
  • The Romanov dynasty ruled from 1613 until the Russian Revolution in 1917.
